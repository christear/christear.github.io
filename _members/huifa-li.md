Huifa Li is an incoming Ph.D. student working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and single-cell biology. His research focuses on developing AI-driven methods for single-cell data analysis, with an emphasis on Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) and Large Language Models (LLMs).

He aims to leverage graph-based representations and foundation models to address a wide range of challenges in single-cell analysis, including cellular heterogeneity, cell–cell interactions, and multi-modal data integration. By bridging advanced machine learning techniques with single-cell genomics, his work seeks to provide scalable, interpretable, and biologically meaningful solutions for understanding complex cellular systems.
